Heart Disease Information

Heart disease, also known as cardiovascular disease, refers to a group of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. It encompasses various disorders such as coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and valvular diseases. Heart disease often develops due to a combination of factors including unhealthy lifestyle choices, genetic predisposition, high blood pressure, high cholesterol levels, diabetes, and smoking. The impact of heart disease is substantial, as it is a leading cause of mortality worldwide. Symptoms can vary depending on the specific condition but may include chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, and irregular heartbeat.

Symptoms
  • Chest pain or discomfort (angina).
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Fatigue.
  • Irregular heartbeats.
  • Swelling in the legs, ankles, or abdomen.
Types
  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): Narrowing or blockage of the coronary arteries that supply blood to the heart.
  • Heart Failure: The heart's inability to pump blood effectively.
  • Arrhythmias: Abnormal heart rhythms.
  • Valvular Heart Disease: Damage or defects in heart valves.
  • Heart Attack: Sudden blockage of blood flow to the heart.
Causes and Risk Factors
  • Smoking, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, obesity, and a sedentary lifestyle increase the risk.
  • Family history, age, gender, and certain medical conditions are also factors.
Prevention
  • Adopting a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and low in saturated and trans fats.
  • Regular physical activity.
  • Managing stress.
  • Quitting smoking.
  • Monitoring and managing conditions like hypertension and diabetes.
Treatment
  • Lifestyle changes.
  • Medications to control blood pressure, cholesterol, and prevent blood clots.
  • Procedures like angioplasty and stent placement.
  • Surgeries such as bypass surgery or heart valve replacement.
