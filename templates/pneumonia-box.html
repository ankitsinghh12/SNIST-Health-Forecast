Schizophrenia Disease Information

Causes of Pneumonia
  • Streptococcus pneumoniae: One of the most common causes of bacterial pneumonia.
  • Haemophilus influenzae: Particularly affects older adults and those with weakened immune systems.
  • Influenza virus: Commonly causes viral pneumonia, especially during flu seasons.
  • Pneumocystis jirovecii: A fungal infection that primarily affects individuals with weakened immune systems.
  • Occurs when foreign substances, such as food, liquids, or vomit, are inhaled into the lungs, leading to infection.
Risk Factors
  • Young children and older adults are more vulnerable due to developing or weakened immune systems.
  • Smoking damages the lungs' defense mechanisms, making them more susceptible to infections.
  • Conditions like chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, diabetes, and heart disease increase pneumonia risk.
  • HIV/AIDS, chemotherapy, organ transplantation, and certain medications weaken the immune system's ability to fight infections.
Symptoms
  • Cough with mucus production.
  • Shortness of breath or rapid breathing.
  • Chest pain, especially during deep breaths.
  • Bluish discoloration of lips and nails (due to insufficient oxygen).
Diagnosis and Treatment
  • Physical examination, including chest auscultation.
  • Chest X-rays or CT scans to visualize lung abnormalities.
  • Blood tests to identify the causative agent.
  • Bacterial pneumonia often treated with antibiotics.
  • Viral pneumonia managed with antiviral medications if available.
  • Hospitalization if severe symptoms, low oxygen levels, or complications arise.
Prevention
  • Pneumococcal and influenza vaccines reduce pneumonia risk, especially in high-risk individuals.
  • Regular handwashing and avoiding close contact with sick individuals.
  • Not smoking and maintaining overall health to bolster the immune system.
  • Taking precautions to prevent inhaling foreign substances.
