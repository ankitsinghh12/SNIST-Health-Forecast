Liver Disease Information

Liver disease refers to a wide range of conditions that affect the normal functioning of the liver, a vital organ responsible for processes such as detoxification, metabolism, and bile production. These conditions can result from various causes, including viral infections (such as hepatitis), excessive alcohol consumption, fatty liver disease, autoimmune disorders, and genetic factors. Liver disease often progresses through stages, starting with inflammation and leading to fibrosis and potentially cirrhosis, where healthy liver tissue is replaced by scar tissue. Symptoms can vary widely, including fatigue, jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), abdominal pain, swelling, and changes in urine and stool color. Early diagnosis and lifestyle changes are key to managing liver disease, while advanced cases might require medical interventions like medication, dietary adjustments, or even liver transplantation.

Types of Liver Disease
  • Alcoholic Liver Disease (ALD)
  • Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)
  • Viral Hepatitis
  • Cirrhosis
  • Liver Cancer (Hepatocellular Carcinoma)
Causes and Risk Factors
  • Alcohol Abuse
  • Viral Infections
  • Obesity and Metabolic Factors
  • Genetics and Hereditary Factors
Symptoms and Complications
  • Early stages might be asymptomatic.
  • Common symptoms include fatigue, jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes), abdominal pain, and swelling.
Diagnosis and Treatment
  • Diagnosis involves medical history, physical examination, blood tests, imaging (ultrasound, CT scan), and liver biopsy.
  • ALD and NAFLD may involve lifestyle changes (alcohol cessation, weight loss).
  • Viral hepatitis might require antiviral medications.
  • Cirrhosis management includes addressing complications and managing symptoms.
Prevention
  • Limit alcohol consumption to recommended levels.
  • Maintain a healthy weight and manage metabolic factors.
  • Practice safe sex to prevent viral hepatitis transmission.
  • Get vaccinated against Hepatitis A and B.
